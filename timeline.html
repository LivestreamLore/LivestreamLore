Livestream Lore - Timeline

Livestream Lore Timeline

2007 – Justin.tv Launches

The platform that started it all, originally just one man's livestream before opening to the public.

2011 – Twitch.tv Spins Off

Twitch becomes the dedicated platform for gaming content, separating from Justin.tv.

2013 – Twitch Plays Pokémon Begins

A crowd-controlled game of Pokémon Red draws over a million participants and becomes a cultural phenomenon.

2014 – Amazon Acquires Twitch

Amazon buys Twitch for nearly $1B, validating livestreaming as a major media force.

2016 – IRL Category Launches on Twitch

The rise of non-gaming content is officially embraced, paving the way for variety streamers and Just Chatting.

2018 – Pokimane Named Twitch’s Top Female Streamer

Imane "Pokimane" Anys becomes a cultural icon and one of the most influential streamers in the world.

2019 – Ninja Moves to Mixer

Top streamer Ninja leaves Twitch for Microsoft's Mixer platform in a high-profile deal.

2020 – Twitch Sees Massive Growth During Pandemic

Lockdowns push creators and viewers to livestreaming in record numbers.

2020 – Mixer Shuts Down

Microsoft announces the closure of Mixer, sending its streamers (including Ninja) back to Twitch or YouTube.

2021 – VTuber Popularity Explodes

Virtual streamers go mainstream with Hololive, VShojo, and independent creators breaking records.

2022 – Subathon Meta Peaks

Streamers like Ludwig and others run marathon subathons, redefining monetization and audience retention.

2023 – xQc Signs $100M Deal with Kick

Streaming deals reach record-breaking levels as Kick competes with Twitch for top talent.

2024 – Twitch Introduces Partner Plus Program

A controversial new revenue program adds stricter eligibility requirements and splits the creator community.
